# Architecture Review: Open-Source Scientific Aerodynamics Research Platform

## TL;DR
- **Build a small, sharp thesis, not a platform.** The defensible bachelor's deliverable is the 2D leading-edge-tubercle study (NACA 0021, Re≈1.2×10⁵, validated against Miklosovic et al. 2004 and the Lohry–Martinelli–Kollasch AIAA-2013-2905 genetic-algorithm optimum) with an airfoil-native surrogate trained on the platform's OWN ~150–500 OpenFOAM RANS runs and a Bayesian-optimization discovery loop. The automotive DrivAer/PhysicsNeMo surrogate path that currently sits in the 16-stage roadmap is a category error for a flapping-wing/riblet specialization and must be removed from the critical path.
- **The five-solver fleet, four automotive surrogates + MoE, full DPW-7/HLPW-5 V&V suite, NeMo agentic layer, and multicloud router are premature for a single developer.** Keep OpenFOAM (+ optionally SU2), defer PyFR/NekRS/JAX-Fluids until the unsteady/differentiable phases actually need them, and run the agentic layer as a thin convenience wrapper, never as a research contribution.
- **The "AlphaFold for aerodynamics" analogy is inspirational but structurally false** — aerodynamics has neither a Protein-Data-Bank-scale curated dataset nor a discrete, cheaply-verifiable output space. The honest, achievable version is a *surrogate-in-the-loop active-learning optimizer over a bounded parametric design space* — which is exactly what GNoME, FunSearch, and AlphaEvolve are under the hood. Budget: ~$50–150/month during the thesis, ~$200–600/month sustained for the multi-year platform, with occasional burst months to ~$1–2k; total stays comfortably under five figures/year.

## Key Findings

1. **Surrogate foundation: train on your own airfoil CFD, not on cars.** Automotive external-aero datasets (DrivAerML, AhmedML, WindsorML) and the PhysicsNeMo model zoo (DoMINO, X-MeshGraphNet, FIGConvNet, Transolver) target a fundamentally different flow regime (bluff-body, massively separated, ~10⁸-cell meshes, pressure/wake-dominated drag) than thin-airfoil attached/stalling flow. A surrogate trained on DrivAer transfers essentially nothing useful to tubercle or riblet physics. The right foundation is the AirfRANS paradigm (Bonnet et al., NeurIPS 2022): exactly 1,000 incompressible OpenFOAM Spalart-Allmaras RANS airfoil cases (Re 2–6 million, AoA −5° to 15°, NACA 4/5-digit), with deliberate full-data (800 train / 200 test) and **scarce-data (200 train) benchmark regimes**.

2. **~200 CFD runs is a defensible Geo-FNO order of magnitude for a bounded 2D design space.** It is corroborated by AirfRANS' explicit scarce-data split (200 training samples) and by GINO (Li et al., NeurIPS 2023), which "successfully trained GINO to predict the pressure on car surfaces using only five hundred data points" with a reported ~26,000× speedup over GPU CFD for the drag coefficient. For a 2-parameter tubercle sweep (amplitude A, wavelength λ) plus angle of attack, 150–500 RANS runs is sound — the surrogate is interpolating within a tightly bounded box, the regime where neural operators are reliable.

3. **Differentiable CFD (JAX-Fluids) enables end-to-end gradient-based shape optimization, but is not needed for the thesis** and is overkill for steady RANS tubercle work. Surrogate + Bayesian optimization (as proposed) is the correct, lower-risk choice for a noisy, low-dimensional, expensive-evaluation problem. Reserve JAX-Fluids/JAX-CFD/PhiFlow differentiability for later unsteady flapping-wing work where adjoint sensitivities over trajectories pay off.

4. **The generative/discovery north star is reachable in honest form.** Performance-conditioned airfoil generation already works in 2D (Airfoil Diffusion, Graves & Barati Farimani 2024; DiffAirfoil; AirfoilGen 2026 with a "valid-by-construction" representation). These proposer models pair naturally with the surrogate as evaluator — the same proposer/evaluator + active-learning loop that defines GNoME, FunSearch, and AlphaEvolve. That loop architecture is the correct analogy; the "discrete crystal lattice / PDB" dataset analogy is not.

5. **Riblets are credibly studyable on a homelab + modest cloud, but ONLY via minimal-span DNS/wall-resolved LES at low-moderate Reτ.** Full-span, flight-Reynolds riblet DNS requires national HPC. The minimal-channel methodology (MacDonald et al., JFM 816, 2017) reduces cost from scaling as Reτ^(9/4) to ks⁺^(9/4), explicitly "feasible … potentially [on] high-end desktop computers." Validated drag reduction: up to ~10% skin friction in the lab for thin-blade riblets at s⁺≈15, and 8.2% for an optimized trapezoidal-groove surface (Bechert et al., JFM 338, 1997), with the viscous-regime optimum at s⁺∈[10,20] (García-Mayoral & Jiménez 2011) — dropping to ~5% at flight Re (Spalart & McLean 2011) and ~0.7–1% fuel burn in service (AeroSHARK).

6. **The provenance contract is excellent and ahead of the curve.** The four-tuple (git_sha, dvc_input_hash, container_sif_sha256, config_hash) + DVC + MLflow + Apptainer is more rigorous than most published computational science and directly defends against the AI-scientist data-leakage failure mode. The gaps are standards-alignment (RO-Crate/W3C PROV, FAIR, Zenodo DOIs, CITATION.cff), not missing rigor. The real risk is over-engineering relative to a thesis deadline.

7. **Orchestration is over-specified.** Prefect 3 + Covalent + a NeMo Agent Toolkit/AI-Q fork + a separate LangGraph/smolagents repo is at least two too many systems for one person. Agentic CFD is real but immature and belongs in a productivity wrapper, not a thesis claim.

## Details

### A. Surrogate / geometry-aware ML architecture (the most important question)

**State of the art (2023–2026).** Geometry-aware neural surrogates for CFD have converged on a few families, each with distinct representation assumptions:

- **FNO / Geo-FNO / GINO.** The Fourier Neural Operator family learns resolution-invariant solution operators in spectral space. Geo-FNO deforms irregular geometry to a uniform latent grid; GINO (Li et al., NeurIPS 2023) couples a graph-neural-operator encoder (handling arbitrary point clouds/SDFs) to an FNO core, is discretization-convergent, and demonstrated car-surface pressure prediction from only 500 training samples (8.31% Ahmed-body / 7.29% Shape-Net-car pressure error) with a ~26,000× drag-coefficient speedup over GPU CFD. Known failure modes: undersampling near sharp corners/high-curvature boundaries degrades local accuracy; cross-attention/message-passing cost grows rapidly with point count.
- **Transformer operators: Transolver, Transolver++, GNOT, OFormer, AB-UPT.** These apply attention over physical tokens/slices. Transolver scales poorly to very large meshes (needs sequence parallelism across GPUs); AB-UPT (Alkin et al. 2025, Emmi AI) is the current automotive SOTA, handling 9M surface / 140M volume cells on a single GPU, trainable in ~a day, via anchored-branched attention with a divergence-free formulation.
- **Graph/point models: MeshGraphNet, X-MeshGraphNet, FIGConvNet, DoMINO.** DoMINO (NVIDIA, arXiv 2501.13350) is the PhysicsNeMo baseline, trained on DrivAerML (500 morphed notchback cars, ~160M-point meshes); it is mesh-independent and decomposes multi-scale fields. FIGConvNet uses factorized implicit global convolutions.
- **Neural fields: MARIO, enf2enf, GAOT.** MARIO (2025) and Equivariant Neural Fields (2025) encode geometry as a modulated implicit field; GAOT (ETH, NeurIPS 2025) combines multiscale attentional GNO encoders with a transformer and outperforms GINO/Transolver/RIGNO on throughput and accuracy, including on AirfRANS.

**Airfoil/wing vs automotive — the central verdict.** The automotive surrogates are optimized for bluff-body, massively separated, pressure-drag-dominated flow on enormous meshes. Thin-airfoil aerodynamics — and especially the tubercle problem — concerns *attached flow, separation onset, and post-stall behavior*, where the quantities of interest (Cl, Cd, stall angle) are exquisitely sensitive to boundary-layer details. Training a surrogate on DrivAer cars and later "specializing" toward flapping wings and riblets is a category error: the learned features (vehicle-wake topology) do not transfer to the relevant physics (laminar separation bubbles, leading-edge vortices, near-wall turbulence). The correct foundation is a **bespoke surrogate trained on the platform's own cheap, bounded CFD runs** over the thesis design space. AirfRANS is the model to emulate: incompressible RANS over NACA 4/5-digit airfoils, OpenFOAM + Spalart-Allmaras, ~200k-point C-grids, ~25 min on 16 CPU cores per case, with explicit 200-sample scarce-data benchmarks. The NeurIPS 2024 ML4CFD competition ran AirfRANS with as few as 103 training examples.

**Minimum data scale.** For a tightly bounded 2–3 parameter design space, 150–500 RANS cases is a sound target. ~200 (the Geo-FNO plan) matches AirfRANS' scarce-data regime; if the surrogate underfits near stall, add cases via active learning rather than blanket sampling.

**Differentiability & shape optimization.** Three routes exist: (1) **surrogate + Bayesian optimization** — sample-efficient, robust to noisy/expensive evaluations, ideal for ≤~10 design variables (the right thesis choice); (2) **surrogate + genetic algorithm** — what Lohry–Martinelli–Kollasch used for the tubercle reference optimum, good for cross-validation; (3) **differentiable CFD or differentiable surrogate + gradient descent** (JAX-Fluids, JAX-CFD, PhiFlow) — powerful for high-dimensional/unsteady problems but with a steep robustness/engineering cost. Neural Concept's DeepGeo (EPFL/SupAero, AIAA Aviation best-student-paper) and DLR/industry work show the gradient route's promise, but it is a phase-3 capability, not a thesis requirement. A recent surrogate-based differentiable pipeline (arXiv 2511.10761) using SDF→marching-cubes→gmsh shows the end-to-end pattern.

### B. The generative / discovery loop (AlphaFold / GNoME analogy)

**The analogy is structurally false and should be reframed.** AlphaFold succeeded because of the Protein Data Bank (a giant, curated, decades-old dataset) and a constrained output (residue coordinates of a known sequence). GNoME (Merchant et al., Nature 2023) succeeded because (i) it bootstrapped from the Materials Project (~69k DFT-relaxed structures in its 2021 snapshot) and (ii) crystal structures live in a quasi-discrete, symmetry-constrained space verifiable by DFT. GNoME's reported results: identification of 2.2 million crystal structures, of which **381,000 are newly discovered stable materials**, with hit rates rising to "above 80% with structure and 33% per 100 trials with composition only, compared with 1% in previous work," expanding the known stable-crystal set from 48,000 to 421,000. Aerodynamic geometry optimization has **neither** a PDB-scale curated dataset **nor** a discrete, cheaply-verifiable output space. CFD verification is far more expensive per candidate than a DFT relaxation, and the geometry space is continuous and infinite-dimensional.

**The honest version of "an AlphaFold for aerodynamics"** is therefore *not* a foundation model that emits novel optimal geometries from scratch. It is the same loop GNoME, FunSearch, and AlphaEvolve actually run: a **generator/proposer** (a performance-conditioned generative model or parametric sampler) coupled to a **cheap evaluator** (the surrogate) inside an **active-learning loop** where a small number of proposals are verified by ground-truth CFD and fed back as training data — a data flywheel. FunSearch and AlphaEvolve (DeepMind) pair an LLM proposer with an automated evaluator and evolutionary selection; AlphaEvolve's headline results (first improvement to Strassen's 4×4 complex matrix multiplication in 56 years; ~0.7% recovery of Google's worldwide compute via an improved Borg scheduling heuristic) all required a *programmatic, ground-truth verifier* that gates hallucinations. The loop architecture transfers; the dataset scale does not.

**Generative 3D for engineering design.** For airfoils, conditional diffusion already works: Airfoil Diffusion (Graves & Barati Farimani, arXiv 2408.15898) generates Cd/Cl-conditioned airfoils; DiffAirfoil uses a latent-space diffusion sampler for aerodynamic shape optimization; AirfoilGen (arXiv 2605.20303, 2026) adds a "valid-by-construction" circle-sweeping representation with performance conditioning; a 2026 study (arXiv 2601.16228) systematically compares coordinate vs PCA vs SDF encodings, finding coordinate encoding best for 2D and SDF the scalable path to 3D. For 3D, the broader generative-design field (point-cloud diffusion, neural-implicit/SDF generation, GLU3D inverse-design lattices) is the relevant literature; CDFAM (Computational Design and Fabrication / AI-driven generative engineering design) is the venue to track.

**Documented AI-scientist failure modes (must be designed against).** Luo, Kasirzadeh & Shah (arXiv:2509.08713, "The More You Automate, the Less You See") identify four failure modes in autonomous AI-scientist systems — **inappropriate benchmark selection, data leakage, metric misuse, and post-hoc selection bias** — found across severity levels in two prominent open-source systems. For an aerodynamics discovery loop these map directly to: training/test geometries leaking through shared meshes; reporting only the surrogate's optimistic Cl/Cd without CFD verification; and cherry-picking the best of many generated candidates. The mitigation is exactly the platform's provenance contract plus a hard rule that *every* claimed optimum is verified by held-out ground-truth CFD before it is reported.

### C. CAD / 3D geometry ingestion, editing, and output

**Mesh vs parametric CAD is a genuine difficulty cliff.** Editing meshes (STL/3MF) in an optimization loop is comparatively easy: free-form deformation, mesh morphing, and SDF-based representations support smooth, differentiable shape changes. Editing *parametric* CAD (STEP/BREP) automatically is much harder — it needs a geometric kernel (OpenCASCADE) and a programmatic modeling layer. The mature open-source options are **CadQuery** and **build123d** (both Python, both on the OCCT/OCP kernel, both export STEP/STL/3MF). build123d is the more Pythonic evolution (stateful context managers vs CadQuery's fluent API); they share the OCP wrapper so objects interchange. Recent Text-to-CadQuery work (LLM-generated CAD scripts, ~69% exact-match on 170k annotations) hints at a generative-CAD direction but is not yet production-grade.

**Best geometry representations for optimization**, ranked by suitability for gradient/generative work: SDF/neural-implicit (smooth, differentiable, scalable to 3D, marching-cubes to mesh) > free-form deformation / mesh morphing (preserves topology, easy gradients) > NURBS/parametric-with-design-parameters (compact, manufacturable, but constrained) > raw mesh (flexible but can self-intersect). Pragmatic recommendation: **optimize in a parametric or SDF space, then emit CAD via CadQuery/build123d** so outputs are manufacturable and reproducible.

**"Visually observable" editing** realistically requires a viewer (PyVista, ParaView, or a three.js/Blender-headless front-end) rendering intermediate geometries/fields per iteration. This is achievable but is pure engineering overhead with no scientific payoff — the user's stated willingness to drop it for thesis scope is correct. Render the *final* optimized geometry and a few key iterations; skip live editing visualization until the platform phase.

**Automated meshing from arbitrary geometry is the single biggest robustness risk.** snappyHexMesh, gmsh, and cfMesh all struggle on never-before-seen, non-watertight, or thin-feature geometry. For the thesis this is bounded (parametric tubercle geometry → scripted meshing is reliable). For the "ingest any user CAD" north star, meshing failure on adversarial input is the dominant practical failure mode and must be sandboxed with validation/repair (watertightness checks, automatic mesh-quality gating).

### D. CFD solver fleet and FSI

**Five solvers is over-scoped for one developer.** Verdict by need:
- **Thesis (2D RANS tubercles):** OpenFOAM alone suffices. SU2 is a reasonable second for cross-validation/adjoint, but optional.
- **Flapping-wing FSI (unsteady, moving boundary):** OpenFOAM + preCICE + CalculiX is the validated open-source path (TU Delft has used exactly this for flexible flapping wings; the Turek-Hron / perpendicular-flap benchmark is the standard gateway). Requires dynamic/overset meshing — a major cost/complexity step.
- **Riblets / near-wall turbulence:** needs wall-resolved LES or DNS, not RANS — a different solver regime; a GPU spectral/FD code (NekRS, or external CaNS/CaLES-class solvers) is appropriate.

PyFR, NekRS, and JAX-Fluids are each justified *only* by specific later phases (high-order/GPU spectral for riblets; differentiable for gradient-based unsteady optimization). Carrying all five behind SolverProtocol now is maintenance burden without thesis payoff. **Keep the abstraction, populate it lazily.**

**preCICE for flapping wing.** preCICE 3 is the right coupling library; the OpenFOAM-CalculiX adapter is mature and used in published flapping-wing FSI. Realistic cost: unsteady moving-boundary FSI with strong coupling is expensive (added-mass instabilities, sub-iterations per timestep, remeshing). Plan it as a multi-month phase, not a sprint.

**Riblets — feasibility and required fidelity (newly sourced).** Validated science: up to ~10% skin-friction reduction for thin-blade riblets at s⁺≈15, and **8.2% for an optimized trapezoidal-groove surface** (Bechert et al., JFM 338, 1997); the viscous-regime optimum sits at s⁺∈[10,20] (García-Mayoral & Jiménez, JFM 678, 2011). At flight Reynolds numbers the achievable reduction drops to ~5% of skin friction (Spalart & McLean 2011), consistent with AeroSHARK's in-service figures. Required fidelity: riblet-resolving DNS needs Δx⁺≤9, Δy⁺≤4, and ≥32 spanwise points per riblet period (Modesti et al., JFM 917, 2021; Choi-Moin-Kim, JFM 255, 1993 is the original). Crucially, the **minimal-span channel** methodology (MacDonald et al., JFM 816, 2017) reduces cost from scaling as Reτ^(9/4) (national-HPC territory) to ks⁺^(9/4), explicitly "feasible … potentially [on] high-end desktop computers." Modern GPU solvers (CaNS, CaLES — up to ~283M cells on a single A100, ~13–19× over a CPU node) make individual low-Reτ riblet cases tractable on one GPU in hours-to-days; published 20–21-case minimal-span sweeps (Endrikat et al. 2021; Modesti et al. 2021) confirm parametric studies are university-cluster scale. **Verdict: riblets are credibly studyable on a homelab + modest cloud IF restricted to minimal-span, low-moderate-Reτ cases; flight-Re full-span DNS is not.**

### E. Provenance, reproducibility, observability, open-source

**The four-tuple contract is exemplary.** Logging (git_sha, dvc_input_hash, container_sif_sha256, config_hash) to MLflow on every run, with DVC + Postgres + MinIO and SHA256-pinned Apptainer/SIF containers, exceeds the rigor of most published CFD/ML work and directly addresses the AI-scientist data-leakage failure mode. The CertificateOfValidity per surrogate (training distribution, held-out error, applicability envelope, expiry) is genuinely good practice rarely seen in the wild.

**Gaps are standards-alignment, not rigor:**
- Adopt **RO-Crate** and/or **W3C PROV** to make provenance machine-readable and portable (interoperable with the wider ecosystem rather than bespoke).
- Mint **Zenodo DOIs** for datasets and model snapshots (FAIR: Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable).
- Add **CITATION.cff** and semantic versioning from day one.
- Track an **ML-reproducibility checklist** (NeurIPS/MLCommons style) for the surrogate.

**Open-source release strategy.** Licensing: OpenFOAM is GPL, JAX-Fluids is MIT, PhysicsNeMo is Apache-2.0. A platform that *orchestrates* GPL solvers via subprocess/container calls (not linking) can itself be permissively licensed, but a copyleft-safe core choice is defensible; settle this early. JOSS is an excellent venue but note its constraints: software must have **≥6 months of public development history before submission**, must be feature-complete, and **pre-trained ML models and notebooks are explicitly out of scope** — so the JOSS paper should describe the *platform/framework*, with the surrogate models released separately (Hugging Face + Zenodo DOI).

### F. Orchestration and the agentic layer

**Consolidate to one orchestrator.** For a single-developer CFD/ML campaign engine: Prefect (Python-native, good ML-iteration ergonomics, dynamic graphs, easy unit testing) vs Snakemake (file-oriented, strong academic/reproducibility community, excellent on single HPC nodes) vs Nextflow (process-oriented, best for cloud/multinode scale) vs Dask/Ray (distributed compute, not orchestration per se). Recommendation: **pick Prefect 3 OR Snakemake and drop Covalent.** Prefect if the workload is ML-iteration-heavy and Python-centric; Snakemake if reproducibility/academic-sharing is paramount and runs stay on the homelab/single cluster. Covalent adds a second mental model for little marginal benefit.

**The agentic layer is premature as a research contribution but fine as a tool.** Agentic CFD is real and improving fast: **Foam-Agent 2.0 (Yue et al., arXiv:2509.18178) achieves an 88.2% success rate with Claude 3.5 Sonnet** on 110 OpenFOAM cases across 11 physics scenarios, vs 55.5% for MetaOpenFOAM and 37.3% for OpenFOAMGPT-Alt, built on LangGraph + Model Context Protocol. **ChatCFD (Fan et al., arXiv:2506.02019, DeepSeek-R1/V3) attains 82.1% execution success on 315 benchmark cases (vs 6.2% MetaOpenFOAM, 42.3% Foam-Agent) and 68.12% physical fidelity** — a metric capturing whether a runnable simulation is scientifically meaningful. The wide spread between these reported numbers itself signals that the systems remain brittle on novel cases. **Verdict:** the NVIDIA NeMo Agent Toolkit + AI-Q Blueprint fork is heavyweight and NVIDIA-ecosystem-locked; the user's other-repo choice of **LangGraph (core) + smolagents (code-heavy sub-tasks)** is better aligned with the open-source/observability requirements and with where the field actually builds (Foam-Agent uses LangGraph + MCP). Run the agent as a thin convenience wrapper exposing MCP tools over solvers/meshers/V&V — do NOT make autonomous agentic orchestration a thesis claim, because the documented brittleness and the AI-scientist failure modes (2509.08713) would undermine scientific credibility.

### G. Scoping and sequencing verdict

**Resolve the tubercle-vs-automotive mismatch decisively: choose tubercles, delete automotive from the critical path.** The tubercle/Geo-FNO/Bayesian-opt path is scientifically coherent, bounded, validatable against two independent references (Miklosovic et al. 2004 wind-tunnel experiment — leading-edge tubercles delay stall ~40% on a humpback-flipper model; Lohry et al. AIAA-2013-2905 GA optimum), and builds the exact muscles the platform needs (own-data surrogate, discovery loop, provenance). The automotive DrivAer/PhysicsNeMo path trains skills and models that do not transfer to the flapping-wing/riblet specialization and consumes the scarce single-developer budget. The roadmap's surrogate path should be **rewritten** to be airfoil-native.

**Defensible thesis slice (bounded, replicable, ~1 person, single GPU + modest CPU CFD):**
1. Parametric 2D tubercle geometry generator (amplitude A, wavelength λ) in CadQuery/build123d → scripted gmsh/snappyHexMesh.
2. 150–500 OpenFOAM RANS runs (validated against Miklosovic 2004 trends and a smooth-NACA-0021 baseline).
3. A Geo-FNO (or lighter GINO/neural-field) surrogate trained on those runs; report held-out Cl/Cd/stall error with the CertificateOfValidity.
4. Bayesian-optimization loop over (A, λ) discovering an optimum; **verify the optimum with ground-truth CFD**; compare to the Lohry GA optimum.
5. Full provenance (four-tuple + Zenodo DOI + RO-Crate); reproducible container.
6. Optional stretch: a conditional generative proposer (Airfoil-Diffusion-style) for a minimal discovery-loop demonstration.

This demonstrates surrogate + discovery + provenance — the whole platform thesis in miniature — without the 16-stage scaffolding.

**Revised multi-year phasing:**
- **Phase 0 (thesis, now → submission):** the slice above. OpenFOAM + one surrogate + BO + provenance. No agents, one solver, no automotive data.
- **Phase 1 (consolidation):** generalize the surrogate to 2D airfoil families (AirfRANS-style); add SU2 for adjoint cross-checks; harden meshing robustness; JOSS submission of the framework.
- **Phase 2 (unsteady + FSI):** OpenFOAM + preCICE + CalculiX flapping-wing FSI; dynamic/overset meshing; introduce JAX-Fluids where differentiability earns its keep.
- **Phase 3 (near-wall turbulence):** minimal-span riblet LES/DNS on GPU (NekRS or CaNS/CaLES-class); validate against Bechert/García-Mayoral.
- **Phase 4 (generative + CAD ingestion):** performance-conditioned generative proposer; CAD ingestion via CadQuery/build123d; the integrated surrogate+generator discovery system; optional visual editing.

**Load-bearing vs premature in the current 16-stage roadmap:**
- *Load-bearing for thesis:* OpenFOAM solver path, one geometry-aware surrogate, the BO loop, the provenance contract, the NACA-0012 walking skeleton, the NACA-0021 validation case.
- *Premature / defer:* PyFR + NekRS + JAX-Fluids (phases 2–3); four automotive surrogates + MoE gating (drop entirely for this domain); full DPW-7/HLPW-5 V&V suite (overkill for low-Re 2D airfoils — use NASA TMR + the specific Miklosovic/Lohry references instead); Dakota + UQpy full UQ (a basic surrogate-error + grid-convergence study suffices for the thesis); multicloud cost router (use one provider); NeMo Agent Toolkit/AI-Q (defer; use a thin LangGraph wrapper later); Covalent (drop).

**Biggest technical risks / likely failure modes for a single developer:**
1. **Scope collapse** — building the platform instead of finishing the thesis. The single highest risk.
2. **Meshing robustness** on novel geometry (the silent killer of "ingest any CAD").
3. **Surrogate over-trust** — reporting surrogate optima without CFD verification (directly the 2509.08713 failure modes).
4. **Maintenance burden** of five solvers + multiple orchestrators + agent stack exceeding one person's capacity.
5. **Riblet/FSI fidelity creep** — attempting flight-Re or full-span work that needs HPC.
6. **Reproducibility theater** — the provenance system consuming time that should go to the science.

## Recommendations

**Immediate (thesis phase):**
1. **Rewrite the roadmap's surrogate path to be airfoil-native.** Delete DrivAerML/AhmedML/WindsorML and the four PhysicsNeMo automotive surrogates + MoE from the critical path. Adopt the AirfRANS paradigm and train on your own ~150–500 OpenFOAM tubercle runs.
2. **Lock the thesis to the tubercle slice (Section G).** One solver (OpenFOAM), one surrogate (Geo-FNO or GINO/neural-field), Bayesian optimization, full provenance, mandatory CFD verification of any optimum.
3. **Cut the fleet to OpenFOAM (+ optional SU2).** Keep SolverProtocol; populate the other four lazily in later phases.
4. **Consolidate orchestration to Prefect 3 alone** (drop Covalent); make the agentic layer a thin LangGraph + MCP convenience wrapper, not a thesis claim. Defer the NeMo Agent Toolkit/AI-Q fork.
5. **Keep the provenance contract; add standards alignment** — Zenodo DOIs, CITATION.cff, RO-Crate/PROV export, an ML-reproducibility checklist. Timebox this so it doesn't eat the science.
6. **Budget the thesis at ~$50–150/month.** ~200–500 RANS cases run largely on the Proxmox homelab CPU; surrogate training is a few GPU-hours on a rented A100/H100. At 2026 specialized-provider rates (H100 ~$1.90–3.29/hr, A100 ~$1.07–1.99/hr on-demand; spot/community lower), even 100–300 GPU-hours of training/experimentation is a few hundred dollars total.

**Multi-year platform:**
7. **Phase the build (Section G).** Deliver the thesis first; add FSI (preCICE + CalculiX) and differentiability (JAX-Fluids) only when the unsteady/gradient phases need them; add riblet minimal-span LES/DNS via a GPU solver in a dedicated phase.
8. **Restrict riblet work to minimal-span, low-moderate Reτ** (MacDonald et al. 2017 methodology) to stay within homelab + modest cloud. Do not attempt flight-Re full-span DNS.
9. **Pursue the generative north star in honest form** — a performance-conditioned proposer (diffusion/VAE) + surrogate evaluator + active-learning loop with CFD verification (the GNoME/AlphaEvolve pattern), not a from-scratch "AlphaFold for aero."
10. **Optimize in SDF/parametric space; emit CAD via CadQuery/build123d.** Sandbox automated meshing with watertightness/quality gating before any "ingest arbitrary CAD" claim.
11. **Sustained compute budget ~$200–600/month**, with occasional burst months to ~$1–2k for a larger surrogate-training campaign or a riblet DNS sweep. Total stays comfortably under five figures/year — far below the "tens of thousands" ceiling.

**Benchmarks/thresholds that change these recommendations:**
- If the surrogate cannot reach useful held-out accuracy (e.g., Cl/Cd error worse than ~5–10% near stall) at 500 cases, **switch from neural operator to a cheaper Gaussian-process / multi-fidelity surrogate** for the thesis.
- If meshing failure rate on parametric geometry exceeds ~10%, **narrow the design space** before scaling data generation.
- If a collaborator or funding appears, **the FSI and riblet phases become parallelizable**; without it, keep them strictly sequential.
- If JOSS is the goal, **start public development now** (≥6-month public history is required before submission).

## Caveats
- **GPU pricing is volatile and source-dependent.** Reported 2026 H100 on-demand rates span roughly $1.90–3.29/hr at specialized providers (Lambda ~$2.49–3.29/hr; RunPod ~$1.90–2.69/hr; marketplace spot lower) up to $11+/hr at hyperscalers; A100 80GB ~$1.07–1.99/hr. Treat the budget envelope as directional and verify live rates at deployment. Spot/community instances cut cost 40–60% for interruptible training.
- **AeroSHARK and the agentic-CFD figures are vendor/airline-/benchmark-reported**, not fully independent. The most independently verified in-service riblet number is Austrian Airlines' (official release, 1 Apr 2026) "verifiable reduction in drag of 0.7%, which can lead to fuel savings of 0.7–0.9% depending on the flight route" — ~930 tons of kerosene saved in the first year across four Boeing 777-200ERs (~830 m² film each). Lab maxima (~8–10%) come from peer-reviewed channel experiments.
- **Per-case CPU-hour totals for academic riblet DNS are not always published explicitly**; the robust feasibility claim rests on the MacDonald et al. (2017) cost-scaling argument plus modern single-GPU channel-flow benchmarks, not on a single quoted core-hour figure.
- **Agentic CFD success rates (88.2%, 82.1%) are on curated benchmarks**, not novel research geometry; real-world reliability on never-before-seen cases is lower and unquantified (e.g., ChatCFD reports only 6.2% for MetaOpenFOAM on its harder set).
- **The surrogate-transfer claim** (automotive→airfoil being a category error) is an engineering-judgment synthesis from the distinct flow regimes and dataset designs, not a single published head-to-head benchmark — though the divergence of automotive vs airfoil benchmark suites (DrivAerML vs AirfRANS) strongly supports it.
